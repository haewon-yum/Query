## Title 1: StoneAge: Pet World (스톤에이지 키우기)

**Launch date:** March 3, 2026 (Android + iOS, global)
**Genre:** Idle/incremental RPG
**Bundle IDs:** `com.netmarble.stonkey` (Android) · `id6737408689` (iOS)
**Moloco iOS UA:** Not activated

### Market Reception

Strong initial chart performance — reached #1 popularity on both Korean app stores within 2 days of launch, peaked at #2/#3 revenue rank in the launch week. Public perception was positive at launch.

### Performance Trajectory

Rapid post-launch deceleration, geographically concentrated in Korea with no meaningful expansion to other markets.

## Title 2: The Seven Deadly Sins: Origin (일곱 개의 대죄: 오리진)

**Launch date:** March 16, 2026 (PS5 + Steam early access) · March 23–24, 2026 (iOS + Android global)
**Genre:** Open-world action RPG, cross-platform (PC / console / mobile)
**Bundle IDs:** `com.netmarble.nanaori` (Android) · `id6744205088` (iOS)
**Moloco iOS UA:** Not activated
**Moloco at launch:** Not activated (budget routed to other channels — confirmed Mar 6 biweekly)

### Market Reception

Strong console/PC launch — debuted #6 on Steam global Top Sellers, reached #1 F2P on PlayStation Store across US, EU, and Asia. Mobile pre-download topped popularity charts in KR, Japan, US, and Taiwan. On paper, Netmarble's highest-profile Q1 release.

### Performance Trajectory

Mobile monetization failed to materialize despite strong install volumes at launch. Market-wide install demand collapsed within two weeks, and budget was cut across all UA channels by late March.

| Week (Mon) | Moloco Spend (USD) | Moloco Installs | All-Channel Paid Installs (Global) | All-Channel D7 Rev (Global) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| W/C Mar 23 (launch) | $127,289 | 36,708 | 1,134,550 | ~$0 (not yet matured) |
| W/C Mar 30 | $37,989 | 6,982 | 163,039 | ~$0 (not yet matured) |
| W/C Apr 6 | $4,885 | 665 | 27,309 | $5,069 + $8 (Moloco) |

*Source: `moloco-ae-view.athena.fact_dsp_creative`, `moloco-ae-view.market_share.fact_market`*

**Key signals:**
- Market-wide paid installs fell **−97%** in 2 weeks (1.13M → 27K)
- Moloco spend dropped **−96%** in 3 weeks
- **Google Play revenue rank #39** just 8 days post-launch (Shala Seo, Mar 31)
- Total all-channel D7 revenue at Apr 6 maturity: **$5K** — near-zero monetization
- Moloco SoI was 3.2% globally (13% in KOR) — consistent with pre-launch routing decision

**Structural issues flagged before launch:**
- 9–16 GB download size creates high friction before first login event
- Business model designed around light, low-ticket purchases — limits ROAS potential
- ADID attribution requirement: Netmarble did not count non-ADID results internally, regardless of delivery

### Budget Collapse

Original UA plan: **~$870K/month** across all channels (shared Mar 5 by Dabin Son)
By Mar 26: **effectively ~$20K** — near-total all-media pullback
Budget was redirected to console campaigns; turned-off geos not being reactivated near-term.

### Internal Netmarble Assessment

> **이승복 팀장 (via Kuyoung Chung, Mar 30):** "Origin — internal assessment is mixed. The business model is light, but revenue is just not registering, and there's a lot of concern about it."

> **Shala Seo (#ads-netmarble, Mar 29):** "It seems like it's underperforming versus expectations."

> **Dabin Son (#ads-netmarble, Mar 26):** "Login CPA was strong, but the overall budget has been cut to about $20K. It's an across-the-board media spend reduction. This Netmarble double launch hasn't produced meaningful results in the short term, which is unfortunate."

> **Dabin Son (#ads-netmarble, Mar 29):** "Would you say Origin failed to generate buzz? The budget itself is way too low even compared to StoneAge…"

> **Shala Seo (#ads-netmarble, Mar 26):** "Seeing TikTok putting more budget into the console campaign, it seems like other platforms are outperforming mobile."

> **Biweekly meeting (Mar 6, pre-launch):** "No Moloco activation this launch — budget going through other channels." *(Netmarble's decision before any performance data existed)*

> **Biweekly meeting (Apr 2):** "Given this is a project where long-term retention matters, proposing a D28 retention/ROAS campaign test. Too early to run immediately given only a week since launch, but aiming for a Q2 test." *(Door not fully closed)*

**Reads:**
- iOS monetizes **35–49% better** than Android — unattributed ARPPU D1 $17.49 vs. $12.97; D7 $24.53 vs. $16.50

- Moloco-attributed ARPPU tracks the Android market baseline well; iOS Moloco ARPPU below market ($13.60 vs. $17.49) — typical when targeting scalable audiences vs. top-whale segment
- Moloco attribution volume is thin (~1.3–1.8% of total installs) — market-wide cohort dominates the monetization baseline

### Key Takeaway for Strategy Doc

Both titles underperformed Netmarble's internal expectations within weeks of launch:
- **StoneAge** is geographically trapped in KR with rapidly decelerating D7 revenue and no approved budget for expansion
- **7DS: Origin** had a structurally weak mobile BM from the start; revenue did not materialize and all-media UA spend was cut by ~97% in under two weeks

The investment reduction is **title-specific UA spend**, not a company-wide pullback — Netmarble as a company is in record revenue territory (2025: 2.835T KRW, +6.4% YoY). The concern is that neither March title generated the monetization ramp needed to justify continued UA investment at scale.

## CTV — Q2 Strategy Pillar

### Current Status: Proposed / In Discussion — Historically Blocked on Measurement

CTV has been actively pitched to Netmarble since December 2024 but has never reached committed scaled execution. One test was run during the Game of Thrones: Conquest & Power pre-registration phase (April 2025) and showed near-zero incrementality. The 7DS: Origin March 2026 launch went to DoubleVerify + Amazon instead of Moloco CTV.

**The single recurring blocker:** Netmarble refuses to rely on MMP probabilistic (view-through) attribution for CTV measurement, demanding deterministic or PA-based attribution instead.

---

### Q2 Unlock: PA Enabled April 2, 2026

**This is the key new development.** PA was enabled on April 2, 2026 — removing the single largest stated blocker. All three historical objections now have answers:

| Historical objection | Answer |
|---|---|
| No PA / deterministic attribution | ✅ PA enabled Apr 2, 2026 |
| No incrementality proof methodology | ✅ Geo-split in 2026 Partnership deck |
| No CTV measurement path | ✅ MMP unified reporting via AF/Singular available |

**Recommended Q2 angle:** Pitch a CTV incrementality test around **Solo Leveling's 2nd anniversary (May/June 2026)** using geo-split methodology, now that PA is live. Confirm test credit availability with Hyejeong. This directly addresses every objection Netmarble has raised since 2024 and leverages a natural seasonality hook.
