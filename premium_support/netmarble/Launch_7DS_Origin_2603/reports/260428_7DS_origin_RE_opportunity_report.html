Section 1

Background — Why RE Campaigns for 7DS: Origin?

7DS: Origin launched on April 6, 2026, and has been running UA campaigns via Moloco since Day 1. As with all mobile RPGs, early retention is the primary value driver — players who lapse within the first 1–2 weeks have already demonstrated engagement intent, which makes them fundamentally different from cold prospects.

A Re-engagement (RE) campaign targets these lapsed users — users who installed and were active, then went quiet — to pull them back before they churn permanently. Unlike UA, RE campaigns work on a warm audience with known behavior history, typically yielding higher conversion rates and stronger revenue efficiency.

This analysis answers the key question: What is the organic revenue potential of lapsed users segmented by inactivity duration? The answer directly grounds the ROAS target and budget recommendation for an RE campaign.

Scope note: This analysis measures organic return revenue — what lapsed users generate when they return on their own, without any push or paid RE activation. These figures are neither a clean floor nor a ceiling for RE campaign returns. Two effects work in opposite directions: (1) RE campaigns increase return rates, bringing back users who would not have returned organically — this adds revenue not captured here. (2) Organic returners are self-selected for higher engagement: they came back because they wanted to. Paid RE returners, nudged by an ad, are likely less motivated and will generate lower D7 revenue per user than the organic figures suggest. Without a holdout experiment separating organic from RE-driven returns, the true incremental value cannot be isolated. Apply a ~30–40% quality discount to the organic revenue/user figures when projecting RE campaign ROAS.
Section 2

Methodology

Cohort Definition

Reference date: April 23, 2026 (today − 7), so the D7 forward window is fully observed as of today. For each user, we identify their last non-install event before the reference date and compute inactivity_days = DATE_DIFF(reference_date, last_active_date, DAY). Users are grouped into daily cohorts (X = 1 to 14) and four inactivity buckets (1–3, 4–7, 8–14, 15+).

Revenue Measurement

D7 revenue = sum of revenue events in [reference_date, reference_date + 7 days). All lapsed users are included in the denominator — including those who never returned — making D7 revenue/user a conservative, campaign-viable ROAS target input.

Attribution Segmentation

Users are classified as Moloco-attributed if any pre-reference event carried moloco.attributed = TRUE. This identifies users who were at some point acquired or re-attributed via Moloco campaigns. All others are classified as Unattributed (organic installs or other channel attribution).

Data source: focal-elf-631.prod_stream_view.pb — unsampled event stream, 100% coverage. User key: device.ifa (GAID, 99.85% non-null on Android). Total lapsed users observed: 101,036 across inactivity days 1–15.

Section 5

Attributed vs Unattributed — Who Returns and Spends More?

Fig 3. D7 revenue/user (left) and return rate (right) by inactivity day, split by Moloco-attributed vs unattributed users.
💡 Insight
Moloco-attributed users are highest-value at very short inactivity, but their value decays faster. On day 1, attributed users generate $18.52/user vs $12.93 for unattributed — a 43% premium. However, attributed revenue/user falls sharply to $5.26 by day 2 and $1.84 by day 3, while unattributed users maintain $9.10 and $8.95 respectively. This divergence likely reflects different user compositions: attributed users who lapse just one day are casual pauses (they came back with their prior intent intact), while attributed users who lapse 2+ days may represent a distinct segment that disengaged more fundamentally. Practically, this suggests two RE sub-targeting strategies: (1) target attributed users inactive exactly 1 day for premium ROAS; (2) target unattributed users inactive 1–7 days for scale and consistent value. Both groups have higher organic return rates than competing inventory at comparable ROAS levels in UA campaigns.
Attribution context: 94% of lapsed users in the 1-day bucket are unattributed (14,476 of 15,370). Attributed users are a smaller but higher-value segment. An RE campaign targeting all 1-7 day lapsed users would predominantly reach unattributed users; for a Moloco-attributed-only RE audience, expect smaller pool sizes but potentially higher ROAS.

Section 7

Open Questions

1

What is the true incremental RE lift? → Holdout experiment required

All revenue figures in this report are organic baselines. We cannot determine how much of the observed return rate and revenue would be driven by an RE campaign vs. natural user behavior. The only way to validate the ROAS case is a properly designed holdout: serve RE to 80–90% of the target audience, hold out 10–20%, and measure the delta in return rates and D7 revenue.

→ Recommended: Design a 2-week holdout experiment on the 4–7 day lapsed bucket before scaling RE spend.

2

Days 10–12 revenue volatility — noise or structural signal?

The day-14 cohort shows an anomalous 12.2% return rate and $0.55/user — far below the day-13 values. Days 10 and 13 show elevated revenue/returning user ($17.00 and $16.73) driven by what appears to be a small number of high-value returners inflating the average. With only 22 days of title history, some cohorts have very small sample sizes and the signal is noisy. These observations may stabilize as the title ages and the lapsed user pool grows.

→ Monitor: Re-run this analysis weekly for 4 weeks to see if the decay shape stabilizes and if the day-14 cliff persists.

3

What re-engagement channels are already active organically?

The analysis found reattribution events in the pb table — indicating some users are already returning via push notifications, organic search, or other channels. Understanding what fraction of the observed organic return rate is already being driven by non-paid channels (e.g., Netmarble's owned push system) would help isolate the addressable paid RE opportunity.

→ Next step: Query reattribution event counts by inactivity day to estimate existing owned-channel re-engagement baseline.

4

Pre-lapse spending tier as a targeting signal

This analysis does not segment by pre-lapse revenue tier (whales vs minnows vs non-payers). The revenue/returning user values in this report are averages across all value tiers. In practice, RE campaigns targeting pre-lapse payers would yield significantly higher ROAS than campaigns targeting non-payers. Identifying and targeting pre-lapse whales (even a small % of the pool) in the 1–7 day window could dramatically improve campaign economics.

→ Next step: Extend the analysis to compute D7 revenue/user segmented by pre-lapse revenue tier (payer / non-payer) using the same pb table revenue events.

5

iOS availability — is an RE analysis warranted for the iOS title?

7DS: Origin has an iOS version (App Store ID: 6737408689). This analysis covers Android only. iOS RE campaigns operate under SKAN/ATT constraints that limit targeting precision and measurement fidelity. An iOS-specific RE feasibility assessment would need to account for IFA availability, SKAN attribution windows, and whether the iOS user base is large enough post-launch to support a meaningful RE pool.

→ Next step: Check iOS pb coverage for bundle 6737408689 and run a parallel analysis if IFA coverage is sufficient.

Queries — Replication Reference

All queries run against focal-elf-631.prod_stream_view.pb (unsampled). Replace reference_date and run date as needed.

Q1 — Lapsed User Cohort + Return Rate by Inactivity Day

Defines lapsed users (had activity before reference_date, none in last 7 days), computes return rate and D7 revenue per user for each inactivity day.

DECLARE reference_date DATE DEFAULT DATE_SUB(CURRENT_DATE(), INTERVAL 7 DAY);

WITH
-- All pre-reference events per user (non-install)
pre_ref_events AS (
  SELECT
    device.ifa                     AS user_id,
    MAX(DATE(timestamp))           AS last_active_date,
    LOGICAL_OR(moloco.attributed)  AS is_moloco_attributed
  FROM `focal-elf-631.prod_stream_view.pb`
  WHERE app.bundle = 'com.netmarble.nanaori'
    AND event.name != 'install'
    AND device.ifa IS NOT NULL
    AND DATE(timestamp) >= '2026-04-06'   -- launch date
    AND DATE(timestamp) <  reference_date
  GROUP BY device.ifa
),

-- Lapsed: last active >= 1 day before reference, no activity in reference window
lapsed_users AS (
  SELECT
    user_id,
    last_active_date,
    is_moloco_attributed,
    DATE_DIFF(reference_date, last_active_date, DAY) AS inactivity_days
  FROM pre_ref_events
  WHERE last_active_date < reference_date
    AND DATE_DIFF(reference_date, last_active_date, DAY) BETWEEN 1 AND 14
),

-- Post-reference activity (did the user return?)
post_ref_events AS (
  SELECT
    pb.device.ifa                          AS user_id,
    MIN(DATE(pb.timestamp))                AS first_return_date,
    SUM(pb.event.revenue_usd.amount)       AS d7_rev
  FROM `focal-elf-631.prod_stream_view.pb` pb
  INNER JOIN lapsed_users lu ON pb.device.ifa = lu.user_id
  WHERE pb.app.bundle = 'com.netmarble.nanaori'
    AND pb.event.name != 'install'
    AND pb.device.ifa IS NOT NULL
    AND DATE(pb.timestamp) >= reference_date
    AND DATE(pb.timestamp) <  DATE_ADD(reference_date, INTERVAL 7 DAY)
    AND DATE(pb.timestamp) >= '2026-04-06'
    AND DATE(pb.timestamp) <= '2026-05-10'
  GROUP BY pb.device.ifa
)

SELECT
  lu.inactivity_days,
  COUNT(*)                                        AS total_lapsed,
  COUNT(pr.user_id)                               AS returned_users,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(COUNT(pr.user_id), COUNT(*))        AS return_rate,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(SUM(pr.d7_rev), COUNT(*))           AS d7_rev_per_lapsed_user,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(SUM(pr.d7_rev), COUNT(pr.user_id)) AS d7_rev_per_returning_user,
  SUM(pr.d7_rev)                                  AS total_d7_rev
FROM lapsed_users lu
LEFT JOIN post_ref_events pr ON lu.user_id = pr.user_id
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY 1

Q2 — Bucket-Level Aggregation (1–3d / 4–7d / 8–13d)

Wraps Q1 as a CTE and assigns each inactivity day to a targeting bucket. Used for Sections 4 and 6.

-- (Wrap Q1 result as `daily_decay` CTE, then:)
SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN inactivity_days BETWEEN 1 AND 3  THEN '1–3 days'
    WHEN inactivity_days BETWEEN 4 AND 7  THEN '4–7 days'
    WHEN inactivity_days BETWEEN 8 AND 13 THEN '8–13 days'
    ELSE '14+ days'
  END                                             AS bucket,
  SUM(total_lapsed)                               AS total_users,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(SUM(returned_users), SUM(total_lapsed)) AS return_rate,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(SUM(total_d7_rev), SUM(total_lapsed))   AS d7_rev_per_lapsed_user,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(SUM(total_d7_rev), SUM(returned_users)) AS d7_rev_per_returning_user,
  SUM(total_d7_rev)                               AS total_d7_rev
FROM daily_decay
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY MIN(inactivity_days)

Adds is_moloco_attributed dimension to Q1. Used for Section 5.

SELECT
  lu.inactivity_days,
  lu.is_moloco_attributed,
  COUNT(*)                                         AS total_lapsed,
  COUNT(pr.user_id)                                AS returned_users,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(COUNT(pr.user_id), COUNT(*))         AS return_rate,
  SAFE_DIVIDE(SUM(pr.d7_rev), COUNT(*))            AS d7_rev_per_lapsed_user
FROM lapsed_users lu         -- from Q1
LEFT JOIN post_ref_events pr ON lu.user_id = pr.user_id
GROUP BY 1, 2
ORDER BY 2, 1
