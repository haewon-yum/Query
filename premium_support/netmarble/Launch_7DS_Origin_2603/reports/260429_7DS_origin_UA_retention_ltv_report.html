  • Retention predicts monetization strongly: I2P climbs from 3.8% (D0-only) → 13.6% (D1-retained) → 28.7% (D7-retained) → 33.9% (D14-retained). Each additional retention milestone adds a measurable conversion lift.
  • ARPU gap widens over time (H3 confirmed): D14-retained users generate $23.04 ARPU at D14 vs $8.68 for D7-retained — 2.7× widening in a single week. The 39% of revenue earned between D7 and D14 is only captured by users who stay engaged past the first week.
  • ARPPU premium is whale-driven: Median ARPPU is similar across segments ($4.9–$5.4). The D14-retained mean ARPPU of $67.9 reflects top-10% payers generating 86% of that segment's revenue. Broad retention improvement will not replicate this premium — it requires targeting whale-prone audiences.
  • Monetization is moderately back-loaded: 25% of D14 revenue is earned on D0, 61% by D7, and 39% arrives in the D7–D14 window. A ROAS lookback shorter than 14 days misses a material share of value.
  • Moloco-attributed users retain significantly better: D1 session retention 26.4% vs ~1.6% for unattributed in the pb data. Moloco campaigns are delivering users with higher early engagement quality.
UA Strategy Recommendation: Retention does not cause higher ARPPU — user quality drives both. Three strategic paths forward: (1) If the goal is to sustain retention quality — extend the optimization window to D28, where long-term retention compounds into higher I2P and cumulative revenue; D28 ROAS data will reveal whether the retained user premium holds beyond the current 14-day observation window. (2) If the goal is revenue maximization — adopt a D28 ROAS optimization target, as 39% of monetization arrives in the D7–D14 window and the tail likely extends further; a lookback shorter than 28 days structurally undervalues retained users. (3) Within the current UA setup — meaningful levers beyond cost efficiency are limited; device targeting and CPI Balancer can improve unit economics, but without a longer ROAS window or retention-based bidding signal, the campaign cannot systematically favor the high-quality users this analysis identifies.

Background & Hypothesis

This analysis tests whether early session retention is a reliable leading indicator of lifetime monetization for 7DS: Origin users. If confirmed, it provides a data-grounded case for optimizing UA campaigns toward user quality — not just install volume.

💡 Interpretation
Median ARPPU is nearly identical across all segments ($4.9–$5.4). The dramatic difference in mean ARPPU (D0-only $21 → D14-retained $68) is entirely driven by a small number of high-value payers. For D14-retained users, the top 10% of payers generate 86% of revenue. This means: (1) the typical retained user does not pay dramatically more than the typical non-retained user; (2) the ARPU@D14 premium of $23.04 is concentrated in the whale tail of the D14-retained distribution. UA targeting for "high retention" will not universally deliver high ARPPU — it will capture more engaged users, but the whale premium requires specifically identifying high-value audience segments.
