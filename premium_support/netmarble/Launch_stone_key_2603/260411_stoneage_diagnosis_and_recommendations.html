TL;DR

StoneAge KOR campaigns are all below the 9% D1 ROAS target. The root causes differ by platform:

  • Android tROAS: CPI exploded to $68 due to audience saturation after a 6x budget ramp. The convertible KOR audience is exhausted at prior spend levels. CPI, not user quality, is the primary bottleneck.
  • Android CPA campaigns: Delivering near-zero revenue ($1–$52 D1 revenue on $5–$10K spend). These are structural ROAS destroyers and should be paused.
  • iOS post-PA: Blended D1 ROAS dropped to 2.1%, which appears to reject the PA hypothesis. But the LAT/non-LAT split tells a different story — LAT traffic unlocked by PA is the best-performing segment at 4.5% D1 ROAS and $12.66 CPI. Non-LAT quality collapsed independently from budget doubling. PA was a net positive.

Primary recommendation: Pause both Android CPA campaigns, separate iOS LAT/non-LAT bidding to concentrate on the 4.5% ROAS segment, and hold Android tROAS at ~$1.2K/day while CVR recovers. iOS is the scale path.

Diagnosis 1: Android CPI Explosion

Primary blocker — Audience saturation + budget over-scaling

The Problem

D1 ROAS  =  D1 ARPU  ×  (1 / CPI)

Current:     3.3%  =  $2.26  ×  (1 / $68.63)
To hit 9%:    9%   =  $6.18  ×  (1 / $68.63)   — need 2.7x ARPU improvement
  OR:         9%   =  $2.26  ×  (1 / $25.11)   — need CPI ≤ $25
  OR:         9%   =  $3.60  ×  (1 / $40.00)   — balanced improvement

At $68 CPI, 9% D1 ROAS requires D1 ARPU ≥$6.18 — 2.7x above current ($2.26). Alternatively, CPI must drop to ≤$25. The realistic path is a balanced improvement on both levers.

Root Cause: IPM Collapse, Not CPM Inflation

IPM (installs per mille) fell 75% from W1 to W4, while CPM rose only 37%. The model is bidding on impressions that don’t convert — a demand-side issue. The convertible KOR audience is exhausted at the prior budget level.

Evidence: Budget as the Primary Lever

Three independent lines of evidence converge:

  1. CVR recovery on budget cut: When Netmarble cut budget 84% ($8.3K → $1.3K/day), CVR doubled and CPI dropped 63% ($90 → $34) — while the CPI Balancer experiment remained active.
  2. CPI Balancer control arm: Both test and control arms of the experiment showed collapsed CVR. The experiment is a minor contributor (~5%), not the cause.
  3. KOR gaming benchmark: Among 396 comparable KOR gaming campaigns (Oct 2025–Mar 2026), Stonekey’s 64% W1-to-W4 CVR decline is at the 12th percentile. Audience saturation is sufficient explanation in the small KOR gaming market.

Current Supply Signals: No Saturation at Reduced Spend

Post-budget-cut: Android win rate improving (29% → 46%), CPM declining ($1.10 → $0.59). No saturation alarm at current ~$1.2K/day. The model has room to recover.

What Moloco Does Well

  • The tROAS campaign I2P (20.09%) is 6x higher than unattributed (3.30%). Moloco finds proportionally far more purchasers.
  • The issue isn’t finding payers — it’s finding higher-ticket payers. The model optimizes for revenue volume, which favors many small payers over fewer large ones.

What the CPA Campaigns Destroy

  • ylgO8XQvDb5nx3k4 (login_1st): 0.34% I2P, $0.19 median ARPPU. These users essentially never spend.
  • yFGQdt2EPPm0NU97 (join_clan): 3.56% I2P matches unattributed, but median ARPPU ($0.96) is 15x below unattributed ($14.70).

Immediate (This Week)

1   Fully pause ylgO8XQvDb5nx3k4 (Android CPA, login_1st)
  • 0.02% D1 ROAS. $1 revenue on $5.2K spend. 0.34% I2P, $0.19 median ARPPU.
  • Frees ~$500/day. Eliminates worst-quality install signal from the model.
  • Risk: Low. These installs contribute nothing to revenue.
2   Confirm yFGQdt2EPPm0NU97 (Android CPA, join_clan) is fully disabled
  • Already winding down ($0 spend Apr 9–10). Confirm disabled in campaign settings.
  • $52 D1 revenue on $10.2K spend (0.5% D1 ROAS). Frees ~$1K/day when running.
  • Risk: Low. Aligns with Netmarble’s own budget cut direction.
3   iOS: Separate LAT and non-LAT bidding strategy
  • LAT users: 4.5% D1 ROAS at $12.66 CPI — 5.6x better ROAS and 18% cheaper than non-LAT.
  • Action: Create a separate ad group or campaign for LAT traffic with a ROAS-oriented goal (instead of CPA login). Maintain non-LAT at current levels until quality stabilizes.
  • Expected impact: Concentrates iOS budget on the highest-ROAS segment. Could improve blended iOS D1 ROAS from 2.1% toward 4–5%.
  • Risk: Medium. LAT sample (335 installs, 7 days) needs continued monitoring.

Short-Term (Next 1–2 Weeks)

4   Android tROAS: Hold $1.2–1.5K/day, re-ramp slowly
  • CVR doubled and CPI dropped 63% on budget cut. No saturation at current spend.
  • Action: Hold at $1.2K/day for 7 more days. Re-ramp at max 20%/day only after CPI <$50 and CVR >0.6%. Do not exceed $2.5K/day without evidence of sustained efficiency.
  • Risk: Low. Netmarble approved $18.8K/day — current level is well within bounds.
5   Validate iOS SKAN ROAS after postback maturation
  • Pre-PA SKAN ROAS 9.6% (mid) was above the 9% target — the strongest signal in this analysis. Post-PA SKAN is 2.1% but likely immature.
  • Action: Re-pull SKAN data once the last post-PA cohort (Apr 9) has ≥7 days of postback maturity (~Apr 16). Confirm AppsFlyer conversion value lock window for this title.
  • Decision gate: If post-PA SKAN ROAS recovers >5% mid, iOS is confirmed as the priority scale path.
6   Quantify CPA–tROAS audience overlap
  • If CPA campaigns bid on the same user pool at $5–13 CPI, they may be cannibalizing tROAS by winning the cheap-to-convert segment. This is currently a hypothesis without data.
  • Action: Query impression-level device overlap between CPA and ROAS campaigns. If Jaccard overlap >30%, cannibalization is proven.
  • Impact: Strengthens the evidence for Actions 1–2 and provides quantitative backing for the Netmarble conversation.

Strategic (May Biweekly Discussion)

7   Propose KPI event change for Android tROAS
  • Current: revenue as KPI action. Model optimizes for revenue volume, producing many low-ticket payers (median $4.04).
  • Proposal: Test adding visit_shop (158K events, highest volume, commercial intent) as a secondary signal. Could shift targeting toward higher-value users.
  • Caveat: Deep-funnel events can starve the model of learning data. Start with the higher-volume event.
8   Position iOS as the scale path for Netmarble
  • Narrative: “Android KOR is stabilizing but CPI-constrained at ceiling. iOS delivered the only segment above 4% D1 ROAS (LAT at 4.5%), and pre-PA SKAN ROAS was 9.6%. Recommend concentrating iOS budget on LAT/PA traffic and validating iOS WW expansion.”
  • The Apr 2 biweekly already noted “iOS WW ROAS test being considered” and “KOR + JPN re-activation in April tied to major update.”
  • CTV angle: PA enablement removes the CTV measurement blocker Netmarble has cited since Dec 2024. If SKAN ROAS matures favorably, propose a CTV incrementality test on Solo Leveling’s May/June 2nd anniversary — directly addressing every historical objection.
9   Correct the ARPPU gap narrative before external sharing
  • Use median ARPPU: $4.04 vs $14.70 (3.6x gap), not mean ($14.95 vs $116.18). Replace “organic” with “unattributed” (we don’t know if those users are organic or from other paid channels).
  • A 3.6x gap with 6x higher I2P = a solvable signal optimization problem, not a platform failure.
