Executive Summary

The notebook is well-structured with a sound ROAS decomposition framework. However, several findings need reinterpretation:

  • The 10x ARPPU gap uses means distorted by whale outliers — the median gap is 3.6x, not 10x.
  • The iOS PA hypothesis — that both MMP and SKAN performance would improve after PA enablement — is not supported by blended data. But the LAT/non-LAT split reveals PA unlocked a segment (LAT) that outperforms the pre-PA baseline on D1 ROAS. The measured decline is driven by non-LAT quality collapse from budget doubling, not PA itself.

Current status (Apr 10): All four KOR campaigns are at reduced budgets. Two Android CPA campaigns are effectively winding down. Android tROAS stabilized at ~$1.2K/day. iOS is the steadiest at ~$1.2K/day. None hit the 9% D1 ROAS target.

Root Cause: Android KOR

D1 ROAS  =  D1 ARPU  ×  (1 / CPI)

tROAS current:   3.3%  =  $2.26  ×  (1 / $68.63)
To hit 9%:        9%   =  $6.18  ×  (1 / $68.63)   — need 2.7x ARPU improvement
  OR:             9%   =  $2.26  ×  (1 / $25.11)   — need CPI ≤ $25
  OR:             9%   =  $3.60  ×  (1 / $40.00)   — balanced improvement

CPI drivers: IPM collapse (75% decline, dominant), budget over-scaling (6x ramp), CPI Balancer (~5%), possible CPA cannibalization (unproven).

ARPU drivers: tROAS finds payers (20% I2P, 6x unattributed) but at low ticket (median $4.04 vs $14.70). Model optimizes for revenue volume, not ticket size.

Root Cause: iOS KOR

D1 ROAS (blended):   2.1%  — appears to reject PA hypothesis

PA was a net positive. Non-LAT collapse is driven by budget doubling + saturation, not PA.
