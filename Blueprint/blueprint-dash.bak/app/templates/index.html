How Blueprint Scores Work
How to navigate this dashboard · Scoring methodology · Pillar composition
▼
① Filter

Use the dropdowns to narrow by region, office, pod, GM or vertical. The search box matches advertiser, app, or campaign name. All scores in the table update instantly.

② Roll-up level

Choose your level of aggregation: Region → Office → Pod → Advertiser → App → Campaign. Scores roll up as a spend-weighted average — a $1M campaign with a score of 20 will drag a region down more than a $10k campaign with a score of 100.

③ Drill down

Click any row to drill into it — the breadcrumb trail at the top left shows where you are. Click any step in the breadcrumb to jump back up. At Campaign level, click a row to open the detail drawer with sub-index scores, detail text, recommendations, and an AI summary.

④ Pillar filter

The coloured pill buttons below the filters let you focus on one pillar at a time. In All Pillars mode the table shows the five pillar-level scores. Selecting a pillar replaces those columns with its individual sub-index scores.

90+ Healthy
70+ Needs attention
<70 At risk
Overall Score

The overall Blueprint score is an equal-weighted average of the 5 pillars (each worth 20%). A campaign must perform well across all pillars to achieve a high overall score — a single strong pillar cannot compensate for a failing one.

Overall = (Data + Optim + Supply + Creative + Duplication) ÷ 5
Pillar Composition
Data & Signals ×0.2
1. Signal Quality — Composite of iOS signal completeness: % IDFA postbacks + fill rates for IDFV, IP, OS version, language, and device model across non-IDFA traffic.
score = IDFA% + (1 − IDFA%) × ScoreLAT
IDFA% = fraction of postbacks (last 3 days, major MMPs only) where the device has a valid IDFA.
ScoreLAT = average of 5 availability rates among LAT postbacks: OSV, IDFV, IP, device language, device model.
Intuition: IDFA is the best signal. If you have it X% of the time, the remaining (100−X)% gets scored on how good the fallback LAT signals are.
DEPRECATED SOON — will be removed from all dashboards.
2. Unattributed Installs — Binary (0 or 100). Is Moloco receiving unattributed (non-Moloco) postbacks? Required for full-funnel signal.
Score: 100 if there is at least 1 unattributed install in the last 3 days of postbacks, 0 otherwise.
3. Probabilistic Attribution — Binary (0 or 100). Is probabilistic/fingerprint attribution enabled for iOS? (Android auto-scores 100.)
Score: 100 if any postback in the last 3 days has attribution_method IN ('probabilistic', 'fingerprint'), otherwise 0. Android always scores 100 (exempt).
Pillar = (Signal + Unattrib + ProbAttr) ÷ 3
Optimisation Strategy ×0.2
4. Budget Mode — Binary (0 or 100). Campaign should be on weekly flexible budget. tKPI campaigns auto-score 100 as an exception.
Score: 100 if the campaign uses weekly flexible budget or daily_custom, or is a tKPI campaign. 0 if it uses fixed daily (is_flexible_budget_spending = false and budget mode is not daily_custom).
5. KPI Event Volume — Scored 0 / 50 / 100. Average daily KPI events at bundle level. Higher event volume gives the model stronger optimisation signal.
CPA/ROAS goals: uses avg_daily_kpi_users_d7 (D7-attributed users per day at bundle level). CPI goals: uses avg_daily_installs.
Thresholds: <5/day → 0 · 5–9/day → 50 · ≥10/day → 100. If fewer than 7 days of baked data exist → defaults to 100 (not penalised).
Pillar = (BudgetMode × 0.2) + (KPIEvents × 0.8)
Supply Maximisation ×0.2
6. Target Supply — Measures the share of accessible bid requests vs total potential. Penalises unnecessary exchange/publisher blocks. Score <95 triggers a supply block flag.
score = accessible_bids / total_bids_in_target_countries × 100
Pulls 3 days of raw bid requests grouped by (country, OS, is_lat, app_bundle, exchange, device_type). A bid is accessible if it passes ALL of: app allowlist/blocklist, exchange allowlist/blocklist, country allowlist/blocklist, device type allowlist/blocklist.
7. Traffic Supply (IFA+LAT) — Measures the share of accessible bid requests vs total potential on missing IFA+LAT traffic.
score = tracking_eligible_bids / total_bids
Same dataset of raw bid requests, assessing only the LAT policy filter: DO_NOT_CARE → all bids eligible → Score 100 · NON_LAT_ONLY → only non-LAT bids · LAT_ONLY → only LAT bids.
8. OS Coverage — Measures whether the advertiser runs campaigns across both iOS and Android. Scored at the advertiser level, then inherited by all campaigns of that advertiser.
The advertiser's iOS/Android spend split is compared to the DSP-wide market benchmark per country (last 3 days).
country_score = 100 × (1 − 0.5 × (|adv_iOS_ratio − mkt_iOS_ratio| + |adv_Android_ratio − mkt_Android_ratio|))
Final score = spend-weighted average across all countries. Intuition: score is 0 if advertiser spends 100% iOS in a 100% Android market.
OS Coverage is informational only — not factored into the supply pillar's overall score.
Pillar = (TargetSupply × TrafficSupply) ÷ 100
Creative Coverage ×0.2
9. Creative Format Adoption — Coverage across video, native image, native video, and HTML formats, weighted by DSP spend share. (×0.30)
For each of 4 formats (VI, NI, NV, RI), compute DSP-wide spend share as weights. Score = 100 × Σ(binary campaign coverage × DSP spend weight). Penalises missing the formats that matter most on DSP.
10. Video Length Mix — Coverage across ≤30s, 31–60s, 61–90s, and 90s+ video buckets. (video sub-score ×0.40)
Same pattern: DSP spend split into 4 buckets; binary per bucket × DSP weight. Only scored if campaign has video spend; else 0.
11. Video Orientation Mix — Coverage of portrait, landscape, and square orientations. (video sub-score ×0.30)
Same pattern across 3 orientation buckets using DSP spend weights.
12. Video End Cards — IEC and SEC coverage. Gaming campaigns only — non-gaming auto-scores 100. (video sub-score ×0.30)
Same pattern across IEC/SEC. DSP spend weight computed only over gaming campaigns.
13. Image Dimension Mix — Coverage of 300×250, 320×50, and 728×90 banner sizes. Only scored if campaign runs image creatives. (×0.25)
Same pattern for 3 banner sizes.
14. Creative Fatigue — % of active creatives not flagged as low-performing or stale for >28 days. Draws data from PSO Creative tool. (×0.20)
score = (total_active − creatives_Rotate_AND_launched_>28d) / total_active × 100
"Active" = had spend in last 7 days. "Rotate" = status is Rotate (Inactive) or Rotate (Low Perf).
Pillar = (0.30 × Format + 0.20 × Fatigue + 0.25 × (0.40 × Length + 0.30 × Orientation + 0.30 × EndCards) + 0.25 × ImageDims) ÷ sum_active_weights
Campaign Duplication ×0.2
15. Duplication — Score 0–100 based on overlap with other campaigns from the same advertiser.

Step 1 — Pair candidates: All pairs of UA campaigns from the same app bundle (product_id) with the same optimisation goal, matching target action, and overlapping active spend dates (last 7 days).

Step 2 — Classify overlap across: countries, audience targets, ad tracking allowance, creative formats, creative groups (full / partial / none).

Step 3 — Assign subtype:
1.1Countries + audience + ad_tracking + formats + creative groups match
1.2Same as 1.1 except creative groups differ
1.3Same as 1.2 except creative formats are partial match
2.1Any country + formats partial + audience not match + ad_tracking match
2.2Any country + formats partial + audience match + ad_tracking partial
2.3Any country + formats partial + audience not match + ad_tracking partial

Step 4 — Bid overlap validation (Type I/II only): For each pair, look at internal auctions where either campaign was in the global top 5 bidders by in_cpm. Bid overlap A→B = fraction of A's top-5 appearances where B was also top-5. If max(overlap_a, overlap_b) <1% → downgraded to subtype 3.4 (false positive).

Step 5 — Score per campaign (worst pair wins):
Type I → 0 · Type II → 25 · Type III → 75 · None → 100.
RE campaigns are exempt, auto-score 100.
Pillar = Duplication score

About the Activation Tracker
What this is · How to use it · What's expected
▼
Activation Overview

Targeted lead list of top advertisers per region based on total campaign spend with suboptimal setups:

  • Campaign duplication — high rates of internal bid overlap
  • Excessive supply constraints

1:1 PSO & GDS client support for lead list customers to resolve and consolidate campaign setups and unblock supply. Salesforce Recommendations will be sent per account with supporting evidence and specific campaign detail.

PSO to collate advertiser product feedback and rationale for campaign duplication and surface to PM. Aim: resolve ~100% of suboptimal setups across the lead list by end of H1 2026.

What you're tracking

For each advertiser the table shows the current state — how many UA campaigns are still suboptimal today, the total spend behind them, and what percentage of their total L7 spend that represents. The objective is to drive that number down to zero.

How to use this tab

Filter by region at the top. Click any advertiser row to expand the campaign-level breakdown with issue detail and MCP links. Use the Note button to log Sales/GDS context, status, and action items — these persist across sessions. The AI Summary tells you at a glance what issues are present and what's recommended.

Issue types
Type I / II Duplication — Two or more campaigns from the same advertiser bidding on significantly overlapping supply (≥10% bid overlap). Causes self-competition and inflated CPAs.
Supply Block — Campaign has a target accessible supply score below 95, typically caused by unnecessary publisher/exchange blocks or missing IFA+LAT traffic.
Note statuses
Not Started Issue identified, no action yet
In Discussion Seller conversation underway
Partly Resolved Some campaigns fixed, others pending
Fully Resolved All issues fixed
Blocked Waiting on seller / external dependency
Temporarily Blocked - Product Need Known product gap; use PSO Validated checkbox
Expectations

GDS should review the tracker weekly with the account's GM. Notes should be kept up to date — log what was discussed, what was agreed, and when it's expected to be resolved. Target: all activated advertisers reach 0% suboptimal spend within 60 days of their activation date.
