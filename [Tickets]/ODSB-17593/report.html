Executive Summary

Account-level C7 CPU degraded 5.3x from $5.72 (Feb) to $30.42 (Apr 2026). The investigation identified three compounding root causes acting in sequence — none of which are Moloco bidding or tracking issues.

Fig 1: Account-level C7 CPU by month. Dashed lines show ticket-reported values.

Factor 1 Campaign Reset

New campaigns replaced mature predecessors on Mar 5, during a 153h action model outage. CPU inflated 2-5x during outage period.

Factor 2 — PRIMARY AIRBRIDGE Attribution Collapse

AIRBRIDGE's own probabilistic matching broke on Mar 21 — both VT (-98%) and CT (-70-90%). Pre-collapse, 70% of MMP installs had no IDFA (AIRBRIDGE used IP+UA from tracking link macros). SKAN installs surged 3-6x as replacement. KB Securities-specific — 34 other advertisers unaffected. Code-verified: Moloco PA setting is irrelevant (no S2S path to AIRBRIDGE).

Factor 3 Budget + Cannibalization

Budget halved in April (-60% iOS, -85% AOS). ISA campaigns created 87% AOS / 38% iOS internal auction collision.

Primary Root Cause: AIRBRIDGE Attribution Collapse (Mar 21)

Fig 3: Moloco attributed installs collapsed while total AIRBRIDGE postbacks remained stable. Moloco share: 11.5% → 0.99%.

Scoping: Isolated to KB Securities

We checked 34 AIRBRIDGE iOS KOR advertisers — none experienced a VT collapse around Mar 21. The aggregate AIRBRIDGE VT share remained stable at 77-83%. This rules out an MMP-level change and points to a KB Securities/agency-side AIRBRIDGE configuration change.

Fig 4: KB Securities VT share collapsed on Mar 21 while all other AIRBRIDGE advertisers remained within normal range.

CPI Decomposition: Genuine Within-Group Degradation

The CPI surge is NOT Simpson's paradox. Every cr_format × exchange segment shows 3-10x CPI increase — the composition shift (KAKAO 58%→68%) is a secondary amplifier.

Fig 5: CPI increased 3-10x across ALL exchanges for first_IOS (Mar → Apr). Not a mix shift — genuine within-group degradation from CVR collapse.

Contributing Factor: Internal Auction Cannibalization

The ISA campaign launch on Apr 3 created significant internal auction collision. However, collision does NOT inflate CPI — AOS CPI actually improved ($2.26 → $2.10) despite 87% collision. The dominant outcome when campaigns collide is both being filtered by bidfloor (58-67%), not internal auction loss.

Fig 6: Internal auction collision rates spiked after ISA campaign launch (Apr 3). AOS pair reached 87% collision.

Hypotheses Ruled Out

Moloco PA auto-disable (PA was never enabled; irrelevant to AIRBRIDGE — no S2S path, code-verified) VT-window-only change (CT also collapsed 70-90% — broader than VT) IDFA availability drop (LAT rate improved from 45% to 24%; IDFA got better, not worse) MMP-wide AIRBRIDGE issue (34 advertisers checked, 0 affected) Simpson's paradox (within-group CPIs all deteriorated 3-10x) Impression engagement/supply change (volumes stable/increasing post-collapse) I2A decline (cohort I2A stable at 14.7-16.6% iOS — reporting artifact) Creative fatigue (all segments equally affected) App-side conversion degradation (total AIRBRIDGE installs stable)

Caveats & Limitations

  • AIRBRIDGE-side trigger unknown: We confirmed the collapse is KB Securities-specific and code-traced the signal path (tracking link macros, not S2S), but the exact AIRBRIDGE configuration change requires advertiser/agency investigation.
  • D7 data maturity: Apr installs after Apr 6 have incomplete D7 cohort data. CPU figures for the most recent week are preliminary.
  • Model training blind spot: Since is_mmp_effective=false (T4), MMP postbacks are excluded from model training (code-confirmed). Model optimized on SKAN signal only, even pre-collapse.
  • IDFA in postback ≠ IDFA at bid time: cv.pb.device.ifa presence tells us what AIRBRIDGE received, not what Moloco had at bid time. The 70% non-IDFA figure reflects AIRBRIDGE's matching, not Moloco's bid-time data.
  • Predecessor CPI context: $0.30-$0.53 CPI was below Korean fintech iOS median ($4-5) but sustained for 8 weeks. Achievable with AIRBRIDGE probabilistic matching active, but not "normal" market rate.
