Origin & mandate

Original problem statement (GDS × Sales workshop, Jan 2026)

"We don't have structural evidence on incrementality, particularly on image banners (e.g. Kakao Bizboard)."

This is the parent question. Everything in this report is downstream of the need to build a defensible narrative — and, where possible, causal evidence — for high-VT campaigns on image-banner inventory in KOR.

Stakeholder inputs that shaped scope

  • Kuyoung Chung: iOS is harder than Android because probabilistic attribution prevents us from measuring incrementality at all. We need a defense logic that works without a clean experimental channel on iOS.
  • Hankhil Lee: For iOS VT inventory, if IB performance looks "too good," the default suspicion is organic poaching (VT credit for installs that would have happened anyway). This is most pronounced on non-gaming titles. A symmetric challenge on the other side: why isn't Android better than iOS, given Android is where we can actually measure?
  • Case archive: "[GDS] High VT cases inputs" sheet is the intake point for specific client cases where sellers face VT pushback. This report should connect to that sheet as the operational tool.

Executive summary

TL;DR — what the observational data shows, and what it cannot.
  • KOR's VT ratio is higher than any other market we measure. KOR-target × all-offices aggregate: iOS 63% / Android 37% (Jan 2026, fact_dsp_publisher). KOR-office × KOR-target: iOS 78.8% / Android 50.4%.
  • KOR over-indexes on IB format. IB is 42% of KOR iOS installs and 27% of Android installs vs global 19.9% / 11.3% (fact_dsp_creative).
  • KakaoTalk is the single largest IB publisher in KOR — 42.3% of Android IB installs, 26.3% of iOS IB installs. VT ratio on Kakao IB = 94.0% (Android) and 97.7% (iOS); non-Kakao IB = 83.5% / 97.6%.
  • KakaoTalk exclusion impact on aggregate VT (KOR office × KOR target baseline): Android 50.4% → 42.1% (−8.3pp); iOS 78.8% → 74.7% (−4.1pp).
  • Exclusive device-ID reach through KakaoTalk (unsampled 2-day snapshot, Mar 28–29 via codered_bid): Android 8.8% of Moloco-reachable IDs are only seen on KakaoTalk; iOS 11.6% (IDFA-consented subset only — iOS figure is directional).
  • Observational user quality (KOR Android, top-10 Kakao-spend bundles, Jan 2026): in the retention heatmap (full 10-bundle view static in §4b) several gaming bundles show VT × KakaoTalk D1/D3/D7 retention comparable to or higher than CT × Non-KakaoTalk baselines. On non-gaming many cells fall below the 50-install threshold and are N/A.
What this data cannot tell us: whether these VT installs are incremental. Observational retention has the same signature whether VT installs are real-new-users or organic-poaching. Only a holdout experiment can separate the two. On iOS, probabilistic attribution blocks the standard experimental path — an alternative proxy design is an open workstream.

Discussion agenda — for GDS Sales sync

Context one-liner. Workshop mandate was "build an incrementality narrative for high-VT KOR campaigns, especially Kakao Bizboard." This analysis establishes composition (IB-heavy), publisher concentration (KakaoTalk) and format-structural VT — but not incrementality. Android can get there via a holdout; iOS cannot (probabilistic attribution). The agenda below is what we need Sales to decide.

Q1. What is the narrative posture we want?

  • Defense-only or offense-and-defense? Are we arming sellers with rebuttals to the three client objections (VT level, user quality, "just exclude Kakao"), or also equipping them to proactively pitch Kakao-heavy spend as a growth lever?
  • Level of hedging. How transparent do we front-load the incrementality caveat? Fully transparent ("we can't prove incrementality today, but…") vs soft hedge ("we observe strong signals that…"). The former protects credibility with sophisticated clients; the latter carries more commercial weight.
  • Audience segmentation. Same narrative for all KOR clients, or differentiated by (a) gaming vs non-gaming, (b) CMO vs performance-marketer buyer, (c) sophistication with MMP attribution? A gaming CMO who already believes in VT is a different conversation than a non-gaming performance buyer who ran an MMP holdout last quarter.
  • Follow-through handoff. When a client pushes past the rebuttal ("run a holdout for me"), does sales have a clear handoff path to DS, and what is the SLA on the experiment?

Q2. Should iOS and Android be separate narratives?

The data shows the two platforms are structurally different — a unified narrative will either overclaim on iOS or underclaim on Android.

  • Android is Kakao-centric, iOS isn't. Android allows "Kakao adds a measurable +10.5pp VT on banner + 8.8% exclusive reach." iOS has no Kakao-specific signal — do we pitch iOS IB as a format-structural story (all banner publishers produce 97%+ VT — see §3a) or a publisher-diversity story (Block Blast + KakaoTalk + KakaoMap + Daum for broad reach)?
  • Operational cost of two narratives. Sellers have finite bandwidth. Is a split narrative workable, or should we calibrate the unified message to the weaker-evidence platform (iOS) and phrase it conservatively?
  • Hankhil's challenge — "why isn't Android better than iOS?" Do we defer this entirely to the Android holdout result, or do we need a holding answer (candidates: KakaoTalk's Android inventory is broader / cheaper / less contested — none tested)?
  • iOS track 2-1 commitment. Workshop listed this as TBD. Do we commit here to an iOS incrementality-proxy design (SKAN pattern analysis / geo-holdout on MMP-attributable / IDFA-consented subset as observational instrument) with an owner and delivery date?

Q3. Why is KOR IB win rate 2–3× other markets? (investigation, not sales-ready)

What we observe (§2d, §2e): KOR Banner win rate iOS 41.2% / Android 28.5% — highest of any top-6 market. Closest peer is GBR iOS 19.7% (half of KOR). Non-gaming IB publisher inventory wins 2× more than Gaming IB (Android 33.2% vs 16.4%; iOS 48.2% vs 28.2%).

What we can't tell Sales yet: whether the high win rate reflects (a) lower competition on KOR banner supply, (b) higher Moloco bid prices, (c) lower bid floors on KakaoTalk/Daum/tmobi inventory, or (d) KAKAO-exchange-specific auction dynamics. None of these has been decomposed.

  • Narrative asset or market feature? If it's a Moloco pricing/targeting strength, it's a sellable claim ("we win 2× more banner in KOR"). If it's a market feature (cheaper floors or fewer bidders), it's not our story.
  • Gate for the P1 narrative? My read: not a gate — the "KOR VT is real because Kakao-exclusive reach is 8.8%" story holds without the win-rate explanation. This can be P2 follow-up.
  • Ownership. This is a supply/pricing investigation, not sales enablement. Candidates: DS + Daniel Jeon (supply) + pricing team. File as a ticket?

Decisions we need out of the meeting

  2. Two narratives or one (Q2) — single go-to-market story vs Android-first / iOS-second framing.
  3. iOS incrementality proxy (Q2) — name an owner and a deadline for the design so "track 2-1" stops being TBD.
  4. Experiment commitment (Q1 follow-through) — can we name the Android holdout pilot advertiser by end of meeting? 1 gaming + 1 non-gaming.
  5. KOR win-rate investigation (Q3) — scope as P2 ticket; assign owner.

3. KakaoTalk's role — VT, reach, assist

Reference: kr_vt_deepdive.ipynb §1–§4 and kakao_deepdive.ipynb. Data window: 2026-01-01 → 2026-01-31 for VT; Mar 28–29 for unsampled reach snapshot. Tables: fact_dsp_publisher, fact_dsp_all, codered_bid, prod_stream_view.imp, prod_stream_view.pb.

3a. Publisher composition within IB

Validation — why VT ratios look uniformly high (85–99%) across IB publishers

The numbers were re-queried from fact_dsp_all (Jan 2026, KOR, creative.format = 'ib') and match exactly — e.g. com.kakao.talk Android 122,954 installs / 94.07% VT; Block Blast iOS 68,219 installs / 98.94% VT. This is not a data-quality issue.

The uniformity is a structural property of IB format + view-through attribution, not a publisher-specific phenomenon. Cross-format VT ratios in the same KOR Jan-2026 window show:

KOR VT ratio by creative.format × OS (Jan 2026, installs ≥ 1,000). Source: fact_dsp_all.

Reads asImage-banner formats (ib, nl Native-List, ni Native-Image) are structurally VT-heavy — KOR Android ib 89.4%, nl 80.1%, ni 61.1%; iOS ib 97.6%, nl 96.7%, ni 91.1%. Click-through-prone formats (vi Video-Interstitial, ri Rewarded, ii Interstitial) are 3–7% VT. So "high VT across IB publishers" is consistent with the format itself, not suspicious per-publisher data.

Why this makes sense mechanically: banner CTR on KOR iOS is typically well below 1% (e.g. Block Blast iOS CT-to-install 0.06% per click). Banner impressions rarely convert via click; most of the installs attributed to a banner happen via the 24h view-through window instead. The two Android outliers — HanaMembers (11.8% VT) and CashMyCharge (29.7% VT) — have CT-heavy install paths and appear as red flags in the chart below.

Section 3 insight (observation only)

  • KakaoTalk is the largest single publisher in KOR IB install volume: 42.3% on Android, 26.3% on iOS (Jan 2026, fact_dsp_all).
  • On Android, Kakao-IB VT ratio (94.0%) is 10.5pp higher than non-Kakao IB VT (83.5%). On iOS, the two are within 0.1pp (97.7% vs 97.6%).
  • Removing KakaoTalk from the aggregate lowers KOR-office × KOR-target VT ratio by −8.3pp on Android and −4.1pp on iOS.
  • In the 2-day unsampled codered_bid snapshot: 8.8% of Moloco-reachable Android IDs appear only on KakaoTalk (2,508,836 of 28,482,109). The Android exclusive-reach-rate among Kakao-reached IDs is 16.8%.
  • iOS exclusive reach rate is 30.3% among IDFA-consented IDs, vs Android 16.8%. iOS total consented device-ID pool (10.3M) is smaller than Android (28.5M).
  • Assist: in a 3-hour window, 0.78% of target-bundle installs had a prior KakaoTalk impression; 0.22pp of that is "assisted" (attributed to a different publisher). This measures exposure-install coincidence only; it does not establish that the KakaoTalk impression caused the install.

Section 3 open questions

  • Are the 8.8% / 11.6% exclusive-reach numbers stable? Snapshot is 2 days; a 30-day window would confirm.
  • What is the magnitude of iOS ATT-driven under-measurement on reach?
  • Why does the Kakao-vs-non-Kakao IB VT ratio gap exist on Android (+10.5pp) but not on iOS (+0.1pp)? Candidate: iOS IB includes Block Blast (27.5% share), a game publisher whose VT behaviour differs from general-purpose apps like KakaoTalk.
  • Should the assist denominator be (a) KakaoTalk-exposed devices or (b) total installs of the bundle? Currently using (b) above for the dark-funnel framing; chart in kakao_conversion_rate_by_window.png uses (a). Both have not been reconciled into a single headline.
  • iOS 30.3% exclusive-reach-rate is higher than Android 16.8%. Whether this is driven by the smaller iOS consented pool, a different underlying app-usage pattern, or the ATT sampling mechanism is not resolved from the 2-day snapshot.

4. User quality (VT vs CT × Kakao vs Non-Kakao)

Reference: kr_vt_deepdive.ipynb §5. Scope: Android only, KOR-targeting, top 10 KakaoTalk-spend bundles per vertical (gaming / non-gaming), Jan 2026. Source: focal-elf-631.prod_stream_view.cv. VT flag = cv.view_through = true. Minimum per-cell install threshold = 50.

Definition — "top-10 bundles per vertical"

Bundles (advertisers) are ranked by gross spend on KakaoTalk publisher supply, separately per vertical. Exact criteria from cell 37 of kr_vt_deepdive.ipynb:

  • Source: fact_dsp_publisher
  • Filters: date_utc BETWEEN '2026-01-01' AND '2026-01-31' · campaign.country = 'KOR' · campaign.os = 'ANDROID' · advertiser.office = 'KOR' · publisher.app_market_bundle = 'com.kakao.talk'
  • Group by: product.app_market_bundle, product.is_gaming
  • Rank by: SUM(gross_spend_usd) DESC, take top 10 per is_gaming bucket (Gaming = True, Non-Gaming = False; NULL bucket excluded).

So "top-10 Gaming" = the 10 game advertisers that spent the most on KakaoTalk inventory in KOR-office × KOR-target Android campaigns during Jan 2026. "Top-10 Non-Gaming" = same within the non-gaming vertical. This is not top-10 by overall Moloco spend or total install volume — a bundle that spent nothing on KakaoTalk (even if it's a top-5 Moloco KOR advertiser) would not appear.

The resulting bundle set is then used for §4a (ITIT), §4b (retention) and §4c (paying rate). Install / retention / paying data is pulled on those bundles from any publisher (not restricted to KakaoTalk), so the four segments (VT×KT, VT×Non-KT, CT×KT, CT×Non-KT) have different sample sizes per bundle — and some cells fall below the 50-install threshold and are N/A.

Section 4 insight (observation only)

  • ITIT: VT segments cluster at median 154–169 min with ~32% within 1h and ~7% within 5min. CT segments cluster at median 0.5–2.9 min with 73–80% within 1h and 56–75% within 5min. The shape difference is consistent with how VT and CT are defined (CT is attributed on click-proximity, VT on impression-proximity within a 24h window).
  • Sample sizes by segment (aggregated across top-10 Kakao-spend Android bundles): VT×KT 97,581; VT×Non-KT 126,205; CT×KT 12,763; CT×Non-KT 136,849. CT × KakaoTalk is the smallest segment (~13k).
  • Retention (sample visible, 10 bundles): heterogeneity is high. Example cells: com.nexon.ma VT×Non-KT D7 27.5%, com.naverfin.payapp VT×Non-KT D7 15.0%, com.ktmusic.geniemusic CT×Non-KT D7 1.7%. No single direction holds across all bundles.
  • D7 paying rate — Gaming: multiple bundles show higher paying rate on KakaoTalk segments vs Non-KakaoTalk within the same install type (e.g. com.actgames.HelloKittyMDS VT×KT 43.0% vs VT×Non-KT 28.5%; com.nexon.ma VT×KT 21.4% vs VT×Non-KT 10.0%). Not all bundles follow this pattern.

Section 4 open questions

  • The retention signature "VT × KakaoTalk ≥ CT × Non-KakaoTalk" on several gaming bundles is observationally consistent with two hypotheses: (H1) VT × KakaoTalk installs are genuinely higher-quality users; (H2) a share of VT × KakaoTalk installs are organic users that would have installed anyway. These cannot be separated with observational data alone.
  • The ITIT pattern (VT median ~160 min) is consistent with both organic installs happening throughout the day and with genuine view-through conversions. ITIT does not distinguish the two hypotheses.
  • Full retention + ARPPU heatmap interactivity requires exporting the 73-row DataFrames to CSV — not currently persisted.
  • Is the gaming-bundle retention finding robust if we restrict to cells with ≥500 or ≥1000 installs? Not yet re-checked.

5. Incrementality narrative crib sheet

Maps to the workshop's "Incrementality narrative on high VT campaigns" scope. Each objection is paired with what the data does say and what it does not. Contribute new live cases to the [GDS] High VT cases inputs sheet.

Objection 1 — "Your KOR VT ratio is suspiciously high."

Yes, KOR-target VT ratio is higher than any other top-10 spend market — iOS 63%, Android 37% (aggregate over KOR-target × all offices, Jan 2026). Observation-backed partial explanation: KOR installs concentrate in IB format (42% iOS / 27% Android vs global 19.9% / 11.3%), and IB has high VT ratios across all publishers; KakaoTalk alone is 42.3% of Android IB installs. We have not measured what share of the total VT elevation is attributable to format mix vs other factors — that decomposition is an open question (§2).

Evidence

§1 VT by country × OS; §2 IB install-share benchmark + supply-side format mix; §3a publisher composition and Kakao IB VT ratios.

Objection 2 — "VT installs are not real users."

On several (not all) top-10 gaming bundles on KOR Android, VT × KakaoTalk D1/D3/D7 retention is comparable to or higher than CT × Non-KakaoTalk. On non-gaming bundles many cells are N/A at the 50-install threshold. This observation is consistent with two hypotheses — "VT users are real" and "Moloco is being credited for organic installs" — which observational data cannot separate.

Evidence

§4 retention heatmap (gaming + non-gaming), D7 paying rate.

Objection 3 — "Just exclude KakaoTalk — it is the problem."

What the data shows about what would be removed: KakaoTalk is 42.3% of KOR Android IB installs (fact_dsp_all), and in the unsampled 2-day snapshot 8.8% of Moloco-reachable Android device IDs appear only on KakaoTalk inventory. The aggregate VT ratio would also fall by 8.3pp on Android (50.4% → 42.1%), which is an accounting change from removing a high-VT segment. Whether those 8.8% exclusive-reach devices would install via other channels without KakaoTalk exposure is an open question that observational data cannot answer.

Evidence

§3a publisher composition, §3b exclude-Kakao VT comparison, §3c reach donut.

Objection 4 — "Why isn't Android better than iOS? Android is the one you can measure."

This is a structural challenge raised by Hankhil Lee. Observationally, aggregate Android KOR VT ratio (37%) is lower than iOS (63%), and Android's KakaoTalk-exclusion delta is larger (−8.3pp vs iOS −4.1pp). We have not run an incrementality measurement on Android yet; the P2 holdout experiment is designed to produce that number.

Evidence — and honest scope

§1 landscape, §3 Kakao by OS. The P2 holdout experiment is designed to produce a causal Android lift estimate that would address this asymmetry directly.
